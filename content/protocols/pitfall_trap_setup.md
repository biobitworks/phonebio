# Pitfall Trap Setup (Ground Arthropods)

## Materials
- Plastic cups (2 nested: outer sleeve + inner collection cup)
- Preservative: propylene glycol (preferred, less toxic) or ~50% ethanol + a drop of detergent
- Rain cover / raised tile, trowel, flagging, GPS

## Steps
1. Dig a hole so the inner cup rim sits **flush with the soil surface** (no lip — insects must fall in).
2. Backfill firmly around the cup so there are no gaps.
3. Fill cup ~1/3 with preservative. Add one drop of unscented detergent to break surface tension.
4. Set a raised cover ~2-4 cm above the cup to keep out rain and bycatch (birds/mammals).
5. Flag the location. Record GPS (wait for accuracy radius to settle) and barometric elevation delta from your datum.
6. Space traps on a grid or transect; record spacing (UWB or tape) and compass bearing of the line.

## Servicing
- Check every **48-72 h**. Strain catch, re-fill preservative, log counts (use shorthand: `n`, `spc`).
- Record weather trend (barometer) — a fast pressure drop means pull or secure traps before a storm.

## Common mistakes
- Cup rim above grade -> catch rate collapses.
- Scented detergent -> repels target taxa.
- No cover -> rain dilutes preservative and drowns non-targets.
