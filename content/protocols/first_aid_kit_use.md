# Using a Basic Field First-Aid Kit (voice-guided, gloves-on)

> Basic field first aid only — not a substitute for professional care. The agent names items aloud because the worker may not be able to read labels.

## Typical basic kit (agent assumes these unless told otherwise)
Nitrile gloves · adhesive bandages · gauze pads + roller gauze · adhesive tape · antiseptic/alcohol wipes · antibiotic ointment · scissors · tweezers · cold pack · triangular bandage · CPR face shield.
*(Basic kits often LACK eyewash and burn gel — substitute clean water/saline.)*

## Voice-guided steps by injury
- **Always first:** put on the **nitrile gloves**.
- **Bleeding cut:** press a **gauze pad** firmly (direct pressure); hold; secure with **roller gauze + tape**; small cuts → clean with a wipe, **antibiotic ointment**, **bandage**.
- **Chemical splash (skin/eye):** **FLUSH with clean water/saline 15 minutes FIRST** — do not seal the chemical under a dressing; remove contaminated gloves/clothing.
- **Minor burn:** cool with **water 10–20 min**; cover loosely with **non-stick gauze**; no ointment on anything worse than minor.
- **Splinter/shallow debris:** **tweezers** if shallow; **leave embedded objects in place** and stabilize around them.
- **Sprain/support:** **triangular bandage** as a sling; **cold pack** for swelling.

## If an item is missing — improvise
No eyewash → clean water/saline · no gauze → clean cloth · no sling → belt/strap/cloth.

## STOP and get professional care / relay for rescue if:
Uncontrolled bleeding · deep/large wounds · **chemical eye burn** · trouble breathing · burn larger than the palm · embedded object · head/neck/back injury · any RED-tier sign.
