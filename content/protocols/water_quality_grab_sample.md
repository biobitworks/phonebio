# Surface Water Grab Sample

## Before you start
- Work upstream to downstream so you don't disturb the next site.
- Note safety: footing, current, cold water. Tell base your location and ETA.

## Steps
1. Label bottle with site ID, date, time. Wear gloves.
2. **Rinse the bottle 3x** with the water you're sampling (unless it is pre-preserved — then do NOT rinse).
3. Face the bottle mouth **upstream**, submerge ~10-20 cm below the surface, fill.
4. For chemistry (e.g., metals, nutrients): leave **no headspace** (no air bubble). For dissolved-oxygen/BOD: follow the DO-specific fill, no bubbles, fix immediately.
5. Record: water temperature, GPS, barometric elevation delta, time, flow note.
6. Store **cool (~4 C) and dark**; deliver within the hold time for the analyte.

## Field measurements (camera-free)
- Temperature, pressure (elevation), and GPS via phone sensors.
- Use the accelerometer to level any meter/probe that requires it.

## Common mistakes
- Touching the bottle interior or cap rim.
- Headspace bubble for chemistry samples.
- Letting the sample warm or sit in sunlight.
