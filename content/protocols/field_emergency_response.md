# Field Emergency Triage (remote, hands-free, ER may be unreachable)

> Field first-response only — NOT a substitute for professional medical care. Get to definitive care as soon as possible.

## 1. First 10 seconds — life safety
- **Fire / smoke:** move away and **upwind**; if a small solvent fire and safe, use a **Class B** extinguisher; otherwise evacuate, shut off ignition/gas if safe, alert anyone nearby.
- **Chemical on skin/eyes:** flush with clean water **15+ minutes**; remove contaminated clothing without contaminating eyes.
- **Inhalation:** get to **fresh air** immediately; loosen anything restricting breathing.
- **Bleeding:** direct pressure.
- Protect your airway; do not become a second casualty.

## 2. Can you reach help right now?
Ask: **can you reach emergency services or your incident lead?**
- **Yes →** call them now. State **location, what happened, exposures, number of people**. Stay on the line if you can.
- **No (no signal / remote) →** stay with PhoneBio:
  1. Continue self-rescue / first aid from the substance's safety sheet.
  2. Move to a **safe, visible** spot; signal for help (whistle, light, bright cloth).
  3. PhoneBio **logs a triage packet** (time, what, exposures, your last GPS) and **relays it to base by text the moment any signal returns**.
  4. Keep talking so PhoneBio can track your status.

## 3. Severity = RED (prioritize getting to care / relaying for rescue)
Unresponsive · severe bleeding · trouble breathing · chemical in eyes · large or spreading fire · suspected fracture/fall.

## 4. Comms tiers when ER is unreachable
- **Voice still works →** call incident lead; PhoneBio can transfer the call.
- **Data down, texts get through →** SMS the triage packet + GPS to base.
- **No signal →** edge device buffers the triage packet; follow self-rescue; it syncs/relays when a link returns.
